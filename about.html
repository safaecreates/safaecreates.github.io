Bio

Safae Lahgazi Alaoui is an African Arab from Morocco where she grew up. She came to the U.S. six years ago to pursue a degree and struggled to choose a practical route over a passionate one. Jumping between classes on CPU and memory to etching a bronze plate in the print studio left her feeling as though there wasnâ€™t enough room to pursue passion. Although in many ways she has always been an artist and will allow it to take many forms in her life.

After getting a BA in computer science with an art studio minor, she moved to Atlanta to live with her sibling. A drive for understanding languages and systems fueled her through becoming a developer. Soon she discovered that although creativity is an everyday part of being a developer, it never satisfied the original call to make her own stories and find her voice.

She is now pursuing the art of story through videography and screenwriting. She participated in the 48 hour film project and has written and directed her own short film, How Many Times.
